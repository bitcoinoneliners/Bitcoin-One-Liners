✦ Quote of the Week

"
Writing a description for this thing for general audiences is bloody hard. There's nothing to relate it to.
— Satoshi Nakamoto July 2010

Why This Quote Matters

By mid-2010, Bitcoin was barely 18 months old. There were no "crypto influencers," no exchange-traded funds, no corporate treasury strategies. Satoshi was trying to explain a genuinely new invention — decentralized, trustless, peer-to-peer money — to a world that had no frame of reference for it. Every analogy fell short: it wasn't quite digital gold, not quite PayPal, not quite a stock.

This one-liner captures the deepest communication challenge Bitcoin has ever faced, one that persists to this day. Sixteen years later, newcomers still struggle to explain Bitcoin at a dinner table. Is it money? A savings technology? A protocol? An ideology? The answer is "yes" — and that's exactly why it's so hard to describe in a sentence.

There's also a beautiful humility here. Satoshi, the architect of a system that would grow to hold hundreds of billions of dollars in value, openly admitted being "better with code than with words." The protocol spoke for itself — the marketing was always going to be the hard part.

If you've ever fumbled through a Bitcoin explanation at Thanksgiving, take comfort: the creator had the same problem.
